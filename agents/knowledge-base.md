# Knowledge Base: Select Publications, Curriculum, Papers & Lectures

## Publications

### 1. "Prudent Investor Standards for Public Pension Funds: A Comparative Analysis of State Laws"
**Publication:** Journal of Pension Benefits | **Year:** 2023

*Synopsis:* This comprehensive analysis examines the variation in prudent investor standards across the 50 states as applied to public pension funds. The paper contrasts the traditional "legal list" approach still used by some jurisdictions with the modern prudent investor rule adopted by most states. Key findings include identification of states with the most restrictive investment limitations, analysis of how state-specific requirements impact asset allocation decisions, and recommendations for harmonizing fiduciary standards while respecting state sovereignty. The publication serves as an essential reference for attorneys and fiduciaries navigating multi-state pension fund compliance.

---

### 2. "ERISA Section 404(c) Protection: What Plan Sponsors Must Know"
**Publication:** Benefits Law Journal | **Year:** 2022

*Synopsis:* An in-depth examination of the requirements plan sponsors must satisfy to obtain liability protection under ERISA §404(c) for participant-directed individual account plans. The article analyzes DOL regulations, reviews significant court decisions interpreting the safe harbor, and identifies common compliance failures that expose fiduciaries to liability. Practical guidance includes a compliance checklist, model participant disclosures, and best practices for investment menu design. The publication highlights the increasingly narrow judicial interpretation of §404(c) protection and recommends enhanced documentation practices.

---

### 3. "Target Date Funds Under Fire: Fiduciary Considerations in Selection and Monitoring"
**Publication:** PIABA Bar Journal | **Year:** 2024

*Synopsis:* This article addresses the surge in litigation challenging fiduciary selection and monitoring of target date funds (TDFs) as qualified default investment alternatives (QDIAs). The analysis covers the DOL's TDF guidance, examines recent class action settlements, and evaluates the key factors fiduciaries should consider when selecting among the numerous TDF families available. Topics include glide path analysis, fee comparisons, the active vs. passive debate in TDF construction, and documentation requirements. The paper provides a framework for defensible TDF selection that balances participant outcomes with litigation risk mitigation.

## Curriculum / Continuing Education Programs

### 4. "ERISA Fiduciary Fundamentals: A Primer for Investment Professionals"
**Program:** SER Annual Conference | **Year:** 2023

*Synopsis:* This foundational curriculum module provides investment professionals with essential knowledge of ERISA fiduciary requirements. Topics covered include the statutory definition of fiduciary under ERISA §3(21), the duties of loyalty and prudence under §404, prohibited transaction rules under §406, and co-fiduciary liability under §405. The program utilizes case studies drawn from DOL enforcement actions and recent litigation to illustrate common fiduciary failures. Learning objectives include ability to identify fiduciary status, recognize prohibited transactions, and implement prudent decision-making processes.

---

### 5. "Performance Measurement and Attribution for Retirement Plans"
**Program:** National Association of Plan Advisors (NAPA) Summit | **Year:** 2024

*Synopsis:* An advanced curriculum designed for investment consultants and plan advisors addressing performance measurement methodologies for retirement plan investments. The program covers time-weighted vs. money-weighted returns, risk-adjusted performance metrics (Sharpe ratio, information ratio, alpha), benchmark selection considerations, and attribution analysis techniques. Practical exercises include calculating and interpreting performance statistics, identifying appropriate benchmarks for various asset classes, and presenting performance reports to plan committees. The curriculum emphasizes documentation requirements for fiduciary protection.

---

### 6. "Public Pension Fund Governance: Best Practices for Board Members and Staff"
**Program:** Government Finance Officers Association (GFOA) Training | **Year:** 2022

*Synopsis:* This governance-focused curriculum addresses the unique challenges facing public pension fund fiduciaries. Topics include board composition and independence requirements, investment policy statement development, manager selection and monitoring procedures, and conflict of interest management. The program examines case studies of governance failures that led to significant losses and regulatory action. Participants learn practical tools for improving oversight effectiveness, including committee charter templates, due diligence questionnaires, and monitoring report frameworks.

## Papers / White Papers

### 7. "Quantifying Damages in ERISA Breach of Fiduciary Duty Cases"
**Forum:** SER White Paper Series | **Year:** 2024

*Synopsis:* This technical paper presents methodologies for calculating damages in breach of fiduciary duty litigation under ERISA. The analysis covers the legal standards for damages recovery, including the "but-for" causation requirement and the distinction between plan-wide and individual participant losses. Methodologies addressed include benchmark comparison approaches, lost opportunity cost calculations, and fee disgorgement analysis. The paper examines how courts have evaluated competing damages theories and provides guidance on presenting damages calculations in expert reports. Statistical techniques for handling data limitations and estimation uncertainty are also discussed.

---

### 8. "The Prudent Expert Standard: Implications for Investment Fiduciaries"
**Forum:** American Bar Association Section of Labor and Employment Law | **Year:** 2023

*Synopsis:* An analysis of the elevated "prudent expert" standard applied to professional investment fiduciaries under ERISA and its implications for standard of care evaluations in litigation. The paper traces the development of the prudent expert standard from common law trust principles through modern ERISA jurisprudence. Key topics include how courts distinguish between amateur and professional fiduciaries, the role of investment consultants and their potential liability, and the standard of care applicable to directed trustees. The paper provides a framework for evaluating whether professional fiduciaries have met their heightened obligations.

---

### 9. "ESG Investing and ERISA: Navigating the Regulatory Landscape"
**Forum:** Pension Research Council Working Paper | **Year:** 2025

*Synopsis:* This timely paper examines the evolving regulatory treatment of environmental, social, and governance (ESG) investing within ERISA-governed plans. The analysis traces the pendulum swing in DOL guidance from the 2015 Interpretive Bulletin through the 2020 and 2022 rulemaking. Topics include the fiduciary framework for considering ESG factors, the distinction between risk-return analysis and collateral benefits, shareholder engagement and proxy voting considerations, and documentation requirements for ESG-integrated strategies. The paper provides practical guidance for fiduciaries seeking to incorporate ESG considerations while maintaining compliance with their fiduciary duties.

## Lectures / Conference Presentations

### 10. "Expert Witness Testimony in ERISA Litigation: Lessons from the Trenches"
**Venue:** PIABA Annual Meeting | **Year:** 2024

*Synopsis:* A practice-oriented lecture drawing on extensive experience providing expert testimony in ERISA breach of fiduciary duty cases. The presentation covers preparation techniques for expert witnesses, effective communication strategies for complex financial concepts, common cross-examination tactics and how to handle them, and lessons learned from successful and unsuccessful testimony experiences. The lecture emphasizes the importance of maintaining credibility through objectivity and adherence to SER Best Practices. Case examples illustrate effective and ineffective expert testimony approaches.

---

### 11. "Detecting Red Flags in Investment Manager Due Diligence"
**Venue:** Securities Experts' Roundtable Annual Conference | **Year:** 2023

*Synopsis:* This lecture identifies warning signs that fiduciaries and consultants should recognize during investment manager due diligence reviews. Topics covered include analysis of track record anomalies, evaluation of operational controls and compliance infrastructure, assessment of key person risk and succession planning, and review of fee structures and potential conflicts of interest. The presentation includes case studies of manager failures that exhibited identifiable red flags prior to loss events. Practical takeaways include a due diligence questionnaire template and red flag checklist.

---

### 12. "State Law Claims in Public Pension Litigation: Beyond ERISA"
**Venue:** National Conference of State Legislatures Pension Forum | **Year:** 2022

*Synopsis:* A legal and practical examination of the remedies available to beneficiaries and stakeholders in public pension fund disputes, which are generally exempt from ERISA. The lecture analyzes common law breach of fiduciary duty claims, state statutory remedies, constitutional protections for pension benefits, and the role of attorneys general in pension oversight. Case studies examine significant public pension litigation including cases involving underfunding, investment losses, and governance failures. The presentation provides guidance for expert witnesses on differences between ERISA and state law standards.

---

### 13. "Investment Policy Statements: Drafting, Implementation, and Litigation Implications"
**Venue:** CFA Institute Annual Conference | **Year:** 2024

*Synopsis:* A comprehensive lecture on investment policy statement (IPS) best practices from both an investment management and litigation defense perspective. Topics include essential IPS components, appropriate specificity levels, the relationship between IPS provisions and fiduciary duties, and common drafting errors that create litigation exposure. The presentation examines how courts have used IPS documents as evidence in breach of fiduciary duty cases and provides recommendations for drafting IPS provisions that establish clear, defensible standards. Sample IPS language and a revision checklist are provided.

---

### 14. "Fee Litigation Update: Trends and Implications for Plan Fiduciaries"
**Venue:** ASPPA Annual Conference | **Year:** 2025

*Synopsis:* An analysis of the ongoing wave of excessive fee litigation against defined contribution plan fiduciaries and its implications for industry practices. The lecture examines recent appellate decisions, emerging theories of liability, and settlement trends. Topics include recordkeeping fee benchmarking, investment management fee analysis, revenue sharing considerations, and the debate over active vs. passive investment options. The presentation provides practical guidance for fiduciaries on fee monitoring practices, documentation requirements, and litigation risk mitigation strategies. Statistical analysis of fee levels across plan size categories is presented.
